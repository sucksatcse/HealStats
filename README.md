**HealthStats** is a lightweight, offline-first Electronic Health Record (EHR) system designed specifically for rural hospitals, community clinics, and frontline healthcare workers operating in resource-constrained environments. While the platform can be adapted for use in other developing regions, its primary focus is addressing the healthcare infrastructure challenges faced by Bangladesh, where many medical facilities still rely on paper-based records and fragmented data management systems.

Healthcare providers in rural areas often struggle with unreliable internet connectivity, frequent power outages, limited technical resources, and low-end computing devices. These challenges make conventional cloud-based healthcare systems difficult to deploy and maintain. HealthStats addresses these issues by leveraging Cloudflare's edge infrastructure and offline-first architecture, enabling healthcare workers to continue accessing and updating patient records even when internet access is unavailable. Once connectivity is restored, data is synchronized automatically, ensuring continuity of care without disrupting daily operations.

The project is research-driven and inspired by the Fast Electronic Medical Record (fEMR) system, an open-source EHR platform presented in *PLOS Global Public Health (2025)*. fEMR was originally developed to support healthcare delivery in humanitarian emergencies and disaster-response scenarios where rapid deployment and operational resilience are critical. HealthStats builds upon these principles while extending the concept beyond temporary crisis management.

Unlike many emergency-focused systems, HealthStats is designed to function as permanent healthcare infrastructure for rural communities. It aims to improve patient data management, reduce administrative burdens, support informed clinical decision-making, and create a standardized framework for healthcare record keeping. At the same time, the system can seamlessly transition into a crisis-response platform during natural disasters, disease outbreaks, or humanitarian emergencies.

This dual-purpose approach is particularly relevant for Bangladesh, a country frequently affected by floods, cyclones, and public health challenges. By establishing structured and accessible medical records before crises occur, HealthStats helps healthcare facilities maintain operational continuity and deliver more effective care when emergencies arise. Ultimately, the project seeks to bridge the gap between everyday healthcare management and disaster preparedness, creating a resilient, scalable, and sustainable digital health solution for underserved populations.
